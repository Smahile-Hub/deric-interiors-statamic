Texture is one of the most underappreciated elements in interior design. While colour and form often steal the spotlight, it is the layering of textures that gives a space its emotional depth and tactile richness.

## Why Texture Matters

A monochromatic room can feel flat and lifeless. But introduce the right combination of textures — a rough linen sofa against a smooth marble table, a plush rug on a polished concrete floor — and the space comes alive. Texture creates contrast, visual interest, and above all, a sense of warmth that paint alone can never achieve.

## The Rule of Three

When layering textures, we recommend starting with three: one smooth, one rough, and one soft. For example:

- **Smooth**: Polished stone, lacquered wood, glazed ceramic
- **Rough**: Exposed brick, raw timber, woven rattan
- **Soft**: Velvet cushions, cashmere throws, linen curtains

## Putting It Into Practice

Start with your largest surface areas — walls, floors, and key furniture pieces. Then build in layers. A rough jute rug beneath a sleek leather sofa creates an immediate tension that feels intentional and sophisticated. Add a soft cashmere throw and suddenly the room feels complete.

The key is restraint. Three to five distinct textures in any one room is usually the right ceiling. More than that and the space begins to feel chaotic rather than curated.
