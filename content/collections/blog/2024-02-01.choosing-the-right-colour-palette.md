Choosing a colour palette is one of the most exciting — and most daunting — aspects of any interior design project. The right colours can make a small room feel expansive, a dark room feel luminous, and a tired space feel entirely new.

## Start With What You Love

Before you reach for a paint chart, start with what you already love. Look at your wardrobe, your favourite artwork, the places you've felt most at ease. Colour preference is deeply personal, and the best palettes come from genuine emotional connection rather than trend-following.

## Understand Undertones

Every colour has an undertone — a subtle secondary hue that affects how it reads in your space. A white paint can lean grey, yellow, pink, or blue depending on its undertone. Understanding undertones is the key to avoiding the classic mistake of choosing a paint that looks completely different on your wall than it did in the store.

## The 60-30-10 Rule

A reliable framework for building a balanced palette:

- **60%** dominant colour (walls, large upholstery)
- **30%** secondary colour (furniture, curtains)
- **10%** accent colour (cushions, artwork, accessories)

This creates a harmonious, layered feel without feeling chaotic.
